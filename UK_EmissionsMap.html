Climate Change

Climate change is a pressing global issue with far reaching consequences and it demands our immediate attention.

“Climate change is real. It is happening right now, it is the most urgent threat facing our entire species and we need to work collectively together and stop procrastinating.” - Leonardo Di Caprio, Actor & Environmentalist

“I’ve starred in a lot of science fiction movies and, let me tell you something, climate change is not science fiction. This is a battle in the real world, it is impacting us right now.” - Arnold Schwarzenegger, Actor & Former Governor of California

According to the Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change (IPCC), the warming of the planet can be attributed to human activities such as deforestation, the burning of fossil fuels, and industrial processes. Changes, such as rising temperatures, extreme weather events, and the melting ice caps, have severe impacts on the population. The World Health Organization (WHO) emphasizes the health risks associated with climate change, not overlooking the potential for conflicts over dwindling resources. Addressing climate change is imperative for environmental and socio-economic stability. In an effort to preserve livelihoods, there is an urgent need to transition into sustainable practices, with a focus on reducing global emissions.

Emissions

Reducing global emissions is paramount in mitigating the catastrophic impacts of climate change, as echoed by numerous experts. Accurate emissions data is crucial for formulating effective policies, with the Global Carbon Project stressing the need for transparent and reliable information to track progress and identify areas for improvement. GHG emissions’ data are fundamental for the mitigation of climate change, It helps to identify the main sources of climatic changes and investigate causal mechanisms, and to monitor progress toward achieving agreed targets. (Busch,et.al, 2023)

Emissions data is statistics regarding the release of Green House Gases (GHG) and other pollutants into the atmosphere. Understanding emissions plays a vital role in tracking an addressing climate change.

The Emissions data provided on the site is for Carbon dioxide and do not consider any other emissions. This serves to highlight emissions within the UK as to help in the decision making process when identifying sites for Carbon Capture and Storage.

Carbon Capture and storage helps to break the link between economic gwoth and carbon emissions as it involves capturing Carbon dioxide (CO2) Emissions produced from power generation and other industrial operations before it enters the atmosphere, and storing the CO2 underground. This has the ability to reduce GHG emissions and aid in the fight against climate change. This technology plays a pivotal role in achieving global climate goals by allowing for the continued use of fossil fuels while minimizing their environmental impact. It is an integral part of the comprehensive strategy required to transition to a low-carbon future and combat the adverse effects of climate change.
